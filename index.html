Explore Islamabad: The Beautiful Capital of Pakistan

Islamabad, known for its modern architecture, lush greenery, and serene environment, is one of the most well-planned cities in Pakistan. The city is home to significant landmarks, including the majestic Faisal Mosque, Daman-e-Koh, and the peaceful Rawal Lake. Whether you're a nature lover or a history enthusiast, Islamabad offers something for everyone.

  1. Faisal Mosque: The largest mosque in South Asia, known for its unique architecture
  2. Daman-e-Koh: A beautiful viewpoint offering panoramic views of Islamabad.
  3. Rawal Lake: A peaceful spot for picnics, boating, and relaxation.

Pakistan and Islamabad: A Glimpse into History

Pakistan and Islamabad: A Glimpse into History Pakistan, officially formed in 1947 after gaining independence from British rule, was the result of the tireless efforts of the Indian subcontinent's Muslims who sought a separate homeland. Under the leadership of Muhammad Ali Jinnah, Pakistan became a sovereign nation on August 14, 1947. Originally, Karachi served as the capital. However, in the 1960s, due to various reasons including its location and population growth, the government decided to build a new capital in a more central region. Islamabad was chosen for its strategic location, nestled at the foot of the Margalla Hills. It was designed as a modern and meticulously planned city by a Greek architect, Constantinos Apostolou Doxiadis, and construction began in the early 1960s. Islamabad represents Pakistan’s forward-thinking vision, symbolizing a blend of natural beauty and urban development. The city was officially made the capital of Pakistan in 1963. Today, it stands as a symbol of political, cultural, and economic progress. While Islamabad reflects modern Pakistan, it is also near the ancient city of Taxila, which dates back to over 3,000 years and serves as a reminder of the region's deep historical roots. The city's landmarks, including the iconic Faisal Mosque, Pakistan Monument, and Daman-e-Koh, are significant to its history and culture. Islamabad continues to evolve as the political and administrative heart of the country, representing Pakistan's aspirations on both regional and global stages.
